Program & Agenda

May 28, 2026 · Hilton Anaheim · 7:00 AM – 4:00 PM PDT

Full Day Agenda

2026 Summit Schedule

Breakfast and lunch provided. 10 sessions delivering actionable insights from California's leading agencies and industry experts.

7:00 – 8:00 AM
Registration & Networking Breakfast

Hosted breakfast while connecting with contractors, agency reps, and partners before the day's programming begins.

8:00 – 8:55 AM
Public Works Summit Plenary Session

Welcome remarks. Setting the stage for the day ahead with insights on California's public works ecosystem.

9:00 – 10:20 AM
Recruitment & Retention Strategies in Construction

Construction faces a workforce crunch—labor shortages, an aging workforce, and 20–30%+ turnover—putting schedules and profitability at risk. To compete, companies must pair modern recruitment with retention: strengthen employer brand, pay and benefits, and a safety-first culture; use technology; and offer clear career paths and inclusive workplaces. Join this important discussion and learn what top industry leaders are doing to keep ahead of these trends.

Emily Cohen · ModeratorBill Boyd · Sully-MillerSarah Tacker
10:30 – 11:30 AM
Session ACalifornia's Water Future

California water providers must deliver safe, reliable, affordable service amid recurring floods and droughts. This discussion explores how smarter planning, approval, and construction of water projects can capture more water in wet years, reduce flooding, and lessen shortages and rate spikes in dry years.

Clay O'Neal · ModeratorCharley Wilson · SoCal Water CoalitionAdan Ortega · MWD (Chair)Susan Kennedy · Cadiz
Session BCalifornia's Infrastructure: Transportation Funding

California's transportation funding conversation is no longer just about where the money comes from – it's also about how the state balances infrastructure needs, public opinion, environmental policy, and long-term fiscal sustainability. In this moderated discussion, panelists will touch on the current mix of federal, state, and local transportation funding, while also examining the broader policy questions shaping the future of transportation investment. The conversation will include alternative funding proposals, voter attitudes toward transportation investment and user-fee funding concepts, and policy issues such as CEQA, VMT, and the challenges of delivering projects in a changing fiscal and regulatory environment.

Gus Flores · ModeratorJon Switalski · Rebuild SoCal PartnershipKiana Valentine · Politico Group
11:30 AM – 12:45 PM
Networking Lunch

Hosted lunch with structured networking opportunities with agency reps and peers.

12:45 – 1:35 PM
California's Economic Future

California's 2026–2030 outlook shifts from rapid, tech-led expansion to steadier, innovation-driven growth. The state aims to lead in AI, clean tech, and aerospace while confronting high living costs, regional gaps, and a cooling labor market—driving more "bottom-up" regional development strategies.

Mark Schniepp, Ph.D. · California Forecast
1:45 – 2:45 PM
Session ACaltrans Mentor Protégé Program

The Caltrans Construction Mentor Protégé Program helps small, disadvantaged, and veteran-owned firms build Caltrans fluency and technical capacity to compete for and deliver Caltrans projects. It pairs eligible protégés (licensed contractors in business at least one year) with experienced mentors through workshops and mentoring sessions.

Abbigail Brown · ModeratorBonny Nyaga · Caltrans, District 4Alexander Santamaria · Santamaria ConcreteAnthony Lino · Sully-Miller Contracting Co.Nicole England · Teichert Construction
Session BSmall Business Participation in Public Works Construction

In California, Caltrans and LA Metro offer opportunities for small, disadvantaged, and veteran-owned businesses through SBE programs, providing mentoring, workshops, and support to help small firms secure contracts for major infrastructure projects.

Gus Flores · ModeratorDavid DeLuz · CaltransLorraine Aldridge · LA Metro
Session CBuilding a Better Safety System

This session covers how to comply with and exceed Cal/OSHA requirements by building a proactive safety system that prevents injuries and protects workers. We will discuss the California Safety Orders and how they differ and often exceed federal OSHA requirements, and how the 1973 Cal/OSHA Act supports enforcement, training, and education.

Victor Sella · ModeratorMichael Donlon · MD Safety Service
3:00 – 4:00 PM
Session AAgency Programs (Caltrans 7, 8, 12)

This panel highlights upcoming infrastructure projects in the Caltrans tri-district region (Districts 7, 8, 12). It is your opportunity to gain a clear view into the vast construction potential of Southern California and get an inside look at billions of dollars of projects coming down the pipeline.

Anna Carno · ModeratorJohn Yang · Caltrans, District 7Christy Connors · Caltrans, District 8Son Nguyen · Caltrans, District 12
Session BAgency Programs (LAWA, POLB, POLA)

This panel highlights upcoming infrastructure projects among Southern California regional agencies. It is your opportunity to gain a clear view into the vast construction potential of Southern California and get an inside look at billions of dollars of projects coming down the pipeline.

Clay O'Neal · ModeratorArmond Morad · POLBShaun Shahrestani · POLA
Session CBuilding Information Management for Infrastructure (BIM4I)

This presentation introduces BIM for Infrastructure (BIM4I): using digital 3D models to plan, design, build, and manage assets such as roads, bridges, and utilities. It focuses on adopting BIM in design and construction and carrying model data forward to operations, maintenance, and asset management to support full lifecycle performance.

Derek Borghi · ModeratorAmy Fong · Caltrans
